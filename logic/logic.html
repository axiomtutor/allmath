Logic

This topic presents the material that one finds in a first-semester course in Symbolic Logic, with particular focus on applications in mathematical proofs, and extended chapters on relevant themes. Here we begin a more rigorous study, unlike Arithmetic which was at times gap-y in order to avoid too much theory.

We define a propositional language and semantics for the language, define concepts and relationships of sentences. We then see the use of Propositional Logic in understanding the working parts of some mathematical arguments. After that we introduce a first-order language and its semantics, which decomposes expressions into finer components. Again we define concepts and analyze the working parts of other mathematical arguments. Finally we introduce a few other themes like proof by induction and second-order logic.

Topics for further study like Modal Logic, Proof Theory, Model Theory, and so on, are reserved for their own later topics.
