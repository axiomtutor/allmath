Arithmetic

Chapter 03 - Extensions, alternatives, applications

Modular arithmetic is extremely important in Math and Computer Science, and studies the remainders of numbers, as if they were arranged in a cycle, like on a circle. p-adic numbers emerge from similar investigations, motivated by the fact that the choice of base-representation can provide information about divisibility. Matrices collect numbers into ordered arrays, with an unexpected definition for multiplication. Sequences refer to sequences of numbers, and series are sums over sequences.

In all cases I try to stick to computation, but at times it is downright impossible to avoid the surrounding theory which motivates these computations.
