In Arithmetic we try to study computation: How to add, subtract, and so on. The emphasis is on number. However, it is not possible to entirely avoid the concepts--like Algebra, Geometry, Probability, Physics, Computer Science, and so on--that motivate studying these calculations. In this regard, things get rough in chapter 03 where the calculations are motivated by concepts found in exotic mathematical and scientific contexts.


The point of this subject is to exercise the basic mechanics of calculation in order to smooth over their introduction and use later on in more abstract settings. Deep understanding is not necessary--perhaps not very possible at this stage--the reader is just expected to warm up to the topics and methods rather than fully internalize them.
